# syntropic-bioergonomics-data

This research investigates the efficacy of a comprehensive framework to analyze and pre-dict musculoskeletal strain by exploring the integration of nonlinear dynamical systems theory with robust statistical modeling. Conventional ergonomic assessment models often rely on linear approximations or opaque machine learning architectures, which may lack the transparency required to account for the stochastic complexity of human motion.
The proposed methodology examines whether nonlinear metrics, specifically Lyapunov Exponents (LyE) and information-theoretic entropy, can effectively quantify local stability and the structural organization of kinematic trajectories. To address common field-data limitations, such as sensor noise and missing values, the study evaluates the application of robust statistical techniques and multiple imputation methods to determine their effec-tiveness in maintaining estimator reliability against outliers. A central objective of this work is to assess the utility of Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) in translating com-plex dynamical deviations into interpretable diagnostic insights. 
By identifying the transition from stability to chaotic instability, this research establishes a scientifically grounded basis for proactive interventions within the novel paradigm of Syntropic Bioergonomics, thereby enhancing both algorithmic transparency and the structural integrity of human-centric industrial systems.
